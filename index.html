About the Chicxulub Asteroid

The Chicxulub asteroid impacted earth within the Gulf of America around 66 million years ago. This meteorite is one of the suspected causes of the extinction of the dinosaurs. The Chicxulub impact crater is one of the largest impact sites on Earth.

Recovery Process

Using radar and sonar sea floor mapping technology, we located the center of the Chicxulub impact site. Using the subterranean drilling vehicle, Virgil (recovered in 2003 near Hawaii), we were able to retrieve the Chicxulub meteorite.

While Chicxulub was originally estimated to be 10-15 km wide, it would appear that millions of years of erosion have reduced its size significantly.

Nevertheless, this is a monumental find for the historic, scientific, and dinosaur communities.
